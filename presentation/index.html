The Structural Blindspot

The "Heatmap Trap" in Traffic Policing

Traditional GIS maps cluster incidents by frequency. But counting infractions ignores the physical layout and structural properties of the road network.

  • No Prioritization: 10 violations on a 4-lane residential side-street flag identically to 1 blockage on a 1-lane main arterial road.
  • Reactive Ops: Tow-trucks chase historical maps instead of predicting live capacity drops in real-time.
  • Cascading Bottlenecks: Small blockages at critical POI access points (like hospitals) lock regional traffic networks.

Mathematical Verification

The Bureau of Public Roads (BPR) Delay Model

To validate heuristic TDI priority, we map coordinate-level violations directly into vehicle-hours lost and commute delay metrics using standard traffic flow equations.

T_congested = T_free × [ 1 + 0.15 × ( V / C_reduced )4 ]
  • Logarithmic Capacity Reduction: $C_{reduced} = (lanes - blocked\_lanes) \times 1500$.
  • Idling Loss Valued: Delayed commuter hours priced at Bengaluru average productivity value of ₹250/hour.
